## Session Log

> Add bullets as the session unfolds. Don’t narrate -- just capture key beats.

- Alarise, Ada, and Helga decide to execute the 2 guards. Ada kills them with a magic missile.
- Lucien wordlessly, coldly, psychopathically executes Crawley with Sorcerous Blast (Cold). His first shot is intercepted by the charmed guard who dies immediately. He makes eye contact with Gimbleblock and rolls a 25 persuasion check. Gimbleblock’s face sets. Lucien fires point blank at Crawley’s head. The blast hits him and encases his head in ice, causing him to suffocate to death.
- Ada rolls a 25 on a history check to see if she knows of a rival thieves guild or organization or individual whose mark she can leave to throw the Myriad off their scent.
	- I have the players invent a rival.
	- They invent a “Gentlemen’s Thieves Guild”, with a “Robin Hood” type leader called “The Tempest.” They are “new kids” in town and trying to demonstrate their cred. Their symbol is a wave wrapping a star. They leave this on a piece of parchment with a single gold piece and a note saying: “For your troubles,” and Lucien leaves it in the safe in Crawley’s office.
- The snipers on the roof start banging on the door and calling for aid.
- Shin approaches the couatl and starts a conversation. Determines that the couatl is of Ioun. It is at the end of its life and has laid an egg. The Couatl instructs them to take its egg if they want to. It also offers to help them remove some of the creatures.
- After determining which creatures are most dangerous (Ada rolled a nat 20 = 28), they ask the couatl to remove the grell and the hellhound. Ada uses Vortex Warp to warp the hippogriff outside. Shin cast Sanctuary on it. It kicked a Myriad member who tried to recapture it and escaped by flying away.
- The party released the fire elemental and fled. Gimbleblock blew up the tunnel on the way out. They returned to his hideout.
- The party gave him 1800 gp to help the neighbourhood and also told him to keep tinkering.
- Gimbleblock gave Shin her Gem of Brightness back, but still encased in the device he made and said that it would be hard to extract it. The party now has a “Gnomish Flashbang.”
- Helga gave Gimbleblock her pistol.
- They divvy up their loot.
- Lucien gave Gimbleblock a letter sealed with his own personal seal and says to present it at the palace if he needs help.
- As they return to the Restless Wharf they encounter a mild festival atmosphere on the streets as people celebrate the destruction of the warehouse. They hear whispers about strange creatures escaping. And they seed rumours about the Tempest being responsible.
- The party goes to the Typhoon’s Rest. Shin, Helga, and Ada take a seat and get food and coffee. Lucien enters and discovers his dad and younger brother sitting and finishing breakfast.
- Lucien’s dad’s favourite boat is called the “Sun Dappler.”
- Shin came over to say hi. Rico doesn’t realize she’s drunk but Carlo does.
- Lucien agreed to go fishing with his dad and little brother tomorrow morning, but they are going to come to the palace for breakfast at 4 am first.
- They engaged in a very awkward conversation, with Shin trying to encourage Lucien and his dad to connect. Lucien expressed (privately, above table) that he feels abandoned and ignored by his dad. Rico, his dad, was clearly trying to connect.
- Lucien and Carlo (his little brother) connected over Carlo catching some fish. Carlo is very excited about going fishing together tomorrow.
- Meanwhile, Alarise snuck into the inn’s second floor and searched a room. She discovered a summoning circle of some kind and called Ada up to help investigate.
- A tough from the main floor followed them but couldn’t sneak up on them. Alarise laid him out in one punch but as she dragged his body into the room she bumped Ada who stumbled into the circle and was teleported to a floating island in the Elemental Plane of Air.
- Downstairs, a fight broke out with three other thugs and Zephyr, who came in at the same time.
- The party killed two of the thugs and forced Zephyr to surrender, while the thug leader, a swashbuckler named Gentleman Jack (not that the party know his name) escaped.
- If the fight had continued, Caliban would have appeared.
- Ada is still stuck in the plane of air, dodging blows.

**Saving Throws:** DEX +6, INT +4

**Skills:** Acrobatics +6, Deception +5, Persuasion +5, Stealth +6

**Senses:** Passive Perception 11

**Languages:** Common, Thieves’ Cant

**Evasion.** On a successful DEX save for half damage, Jack takes no damage. On a failure, half.

**Fancy Footwork.** After making a melee attack against a creature, Jack doesn’t provoke opportunity attacks from that creature for the rest of the turn, hit or miss.

**Cunning Action.** Dash, Disengage, or Hide.

##### Reactions

**Riposte (Recharge 5–6).** When a creature misses Jack with a melee attack, he can make one Rapier attack against that creature.

> **Tactics:** Jack uses Fancy Footwork to hit and move without provoking. He focuses Sneak Attack on the squishiest target, uses Cunning Action to reposition, and saves Riposte for when he’s cornered. He’ll flee if reduced below 15 HP.

**Multiattack.** The elemental makes three Touch attacks.

**Eruption (Recharge 5–6).** The elemental releases a burst of flame. Each creature within 15 feet must make a DC 14 DEX saving throw, taking 21 (6d6) fire damage on a failure, or half on a success. Flammable objects in the area that aren’t being worn or carried ignite.

> **Tactics:** This thing is a wrecking ball. It charges into melee clusters, relying on Fire Form passive damage to punish anyone nearby. Uses Eruption when it can catch 3+ targets. It’s indiscriminate — it’s an elemental force of destruction, not a tactician. **This is the real threat in this encounter for a level 4 party.**

# Encounter Notes

**Difficulty:** This is a **deadly** encounter for a level 4 party of 5, even before accounting for Ada being removed. The Greater Fire Elemental alone is a serious problem.

**Faction Dynamics (suggested):**

- **Jack + Toughs:** One faction. Jack is the villain; the toughs are his muscle.
- **Caliban + Zephyr + Air Elementals:** Second faction. Their allegiance to the party is unclear — they serve Caliban’s interests.
- **Greater Fire Elemental:** Wild card. Likely hostile to everyone, or possibly drawn through a rift alongside/because of the planar breach that took Ada.
- **Party:** Down one member (Ada on Elemental Plane of Air).

**Key Questions for the DM:**

1. Is the Greater Fire Elemental hostile to all, or was it summoned by someone?
2. Is Caliban friendly, neutral, or hostile to the party?
3. What’s the path to getting Ada back? Is the planar breach still open?
4. Will Gentleman Jack try to flee, or does he have a reason to stay and fight?
