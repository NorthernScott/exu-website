## Quick Reference

> Copy these from the prep file at session start. Keep this section compact -- it is your at-a-glance cheat sheet during play.

**Strong Start:** The party made their way to the Skew to find the second location that Drazz directed them to.

**Secrets/Clues to Watch For:**

1. The Warehouse is controlled by the Myriad and led by an evil gnome named Crawley.
2. The Myriad is trafficking in extra-planar and magical creatures that are leaking into the material plane (presumably because of the post-apogee solstice change in divinity). The warehouse contains:
	1. Coatl of Ioun (which has laid an egg that the party can take)
	2. Swarm of Dretches
	3. 1+ Grick
	4. 1+ Grell
	5. Swarm of Larvae (demonic)
	6. 1 Hippogriff
	7. A crate of Empyrean Iotas
	8. Assorted Imps
	9. A magic mirror that the Myriad uses for remote communication (and could be used by the Myriad to identify the party if they are exposed to it)

**Combat Outs:**

- The party could successfully ambush the warehouse.
- The party could negotiate with Crawley, but that would be difficult.
- If they get into a full-on fight with the Myriad they risk being overwhelmed.
- If they release all of the creatures in the warehouse they risk causing chaos in the city.

## Session Log

> Add bullets as the session unfolds. Don’t narrate -- just capture key beats.

- On their way to the warehouse, they were accosted by a group of street thugs led by a human tough who identified himself as “Mirt.”
- They learned that the thugs were part of The Myriad (thieves guild), and they were warned to stay away from the neighbourhood.
- They learned that the man who runs the warehouse and who leads the Myriad in the Skew is a gnome named Crawley.
- The party engaged in some minor combat but mostly distracted them and escaped.
- About a block away, they were pulled into a doorway by a gnomish man named [[Gimbleblock Turnscrew]].
- Gimbleblock is an artificer, and told them them that he used to know Crawley. He thinks that Crawley is beyond saving because “he doesn’t tinker.”
- Gimbleblock offered to help them take down the warehouse because he’s tired of his neighbourhood being riddled by crime.
- After some preparation, the party - led by Gimbleblock - snuck to the warehouse through a series of sewers, tunnels, basements, and alleys. They successfully snuck into the warehouse, defeated the guards, and pacified Crawley, all without alerting the rest of the Myriad agents in the area around the warehouse.
- At the end of the session they had looted the warehouse, realized it was full of planar creatures, and knew that eventually the Myriad patrols in the area would come to check-in.
- They found the magic mirror in a safe but DID NOT show their faces, so the Myriad does not know who they are.
- They had not yet lit the lantern that Drazz gave them and that released the air elemental in the Sluiceweave.
- They found 9000 gp + gems and some assorted stuff

## What Worked / What Didn’t

**Worked well:**

- Having an NPC help the party plan and give them informationj.
- Kicking butt, making a plan, and actually executing the plan flawlessly.
- The first conflict with Mirt was good and forced the party to retreat, which is a good experience for them to have.
- The pacing of the fight was great.

**Didn’t land:**

- I felt like the collection of the loot was forced and took too long. Partly because I hadn’t planned it out and partly because it was late in the session and I was trying to wrap up.
- Having Gimblebock confront Crawley (along with the “suggested” guard) felt anticlimactic to me, but was also narratively appropriate for Gimblebock.
- Because of timing, I had to defer one of the major plot beats to next session: figuring out how to deal with all of the extra-planar creatures in the warehouse since Drazz’s elemental will likely destroy it.

**Would do differently:**

- Try to move through the early part of the session faster.
