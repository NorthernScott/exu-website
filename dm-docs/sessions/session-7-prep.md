## 2. Strong Start

### Mob at the Carnival

A frightened mob of 30-40 people is attacking the carnival. They’re throwing rocks, torches, overturning carts. The mood is volatile -- fear from the tsunami has turned into anger.

**Mob Leaders:**

- **Grevus Tann** (human fisherman, 40s): Lost his boat in the tsunami. Shouting “The carnival brought this curse! Strangers and freaks!”
- **Marta Kiel** (half-elf merchant, 50s): Her warehouse was damaged. More calculating -- sees opportunity to drive out competition.

**The Pantomime Pack** (tabaxi vampire clowns) are visible trying to escape through the back of the carnival grounds. The mob doesn’t know they’re vampires -- they just see “outsiders” fleeing.

**Resolution Options:**

**Calm the Mob:**

- **Persuasion DC 16** (with advantage if they invoke the fact that the carnival helped refugees)
- **Intimidation DC 14** (but risks escalation if failed)
- **Performance DC 15** (distraction/spectacle to diffuse tension)

**Let It Play Out:**

- Carnival sustains significant damage (~500gp)
- Pantomime Pack escapes successfully
- Brun is injured protecting refugees
- Party’s reputation with carnival folk improves

**Help the Pack Escape:**

- Creates distraction for vampires
- Pack may remember this later
- Mob still needs to be dealt with afterward

**Stop the Pack:**

- Pack doesn’t fight back, just tries to flee
- Will be captured by city guard who arrive 2 rounds later
- Skrang might appear to help them escape

**Aftermath:**

City guard (led by **Watch Captain Doria Venn**) arrives regardless. She’s exhausted, overworked post-tsunami. Disperses mob, takes statements.

If party helped: Doria thanks them, mentions the guard is stretched thin. “Strange things in the city lately.”

## 4. Secrets and Clues

1. Caliban is a djinn who came to Nicodranas approximately 6 years ago, building influence through smuggling (warehouse), siphoning magical energy (Sluiceweave), and an information network (tavern).
2. Caliban’s goal is to gather enough power/resources to break free of an ancient binding. He needs magical artifacts (smuggling), raw magical energy (Sluiceweave ley line), and information about planar weaknesses in the city.
3. Caliban’s 6-year energy drain weakened the city’s ancient magical defenses, making the tsunami far worse than it should have been.
4. U’kotoa actually stopped the tsunami by creating the offshore barrier. This gives U’kotoa credit, worship, and influence -- the Kuo-Toa priests were already worshipping it, and more people are unknowingly attributing their survival to “the sea itself.”
5. Dazz (as a fey entity) is trying to maintain balance and prevent Caliban from causing further catastrophe. He’s also worried about U’kotoa gaining influence through “saving” the city.
6. The ley line anchor (ancient pillar in the Sluiceweave) is pre-Divergence construction, part of the city’s original magical infrastructure. Primordial inscription: “Four winds carry, four waters flow, one heart protects all.”
7. Renz Kallow (warehouse manager) is trapped -- never met “Mr. Telban” personally, only deals with “Mr. Stormcaller” (Caliban’s alias) and Zephyr. Payment comes in gold bars stamped with City of Brass symbols.
8. Mara (warehouse guard) is secretly reporting to “a contact at the Palace” -- she’s being paid to keep tabs on Caliban’s operation but not interfere.
9. Rasha al-Nadir (air genasi woman at tavern) is a Djinn Hunter sent to capture Caliban for crimes in the City of Brass. She wants to capture him, not kill him -- potential conflict with Dazz’s mission.
10. The Marquess (Lucien’s mother) is a secret follower/warlock of U’kotoa. She WANTS U’kotoa to gain power and influence. She’s been watching Caliban’s operation through Mara. She supports stopping Caliban because his job is done, he’s a loose end, and removing him preserves U’kotoa’s credit for saving the city.

## 5. Fantastic Locations

**Warehouse in the Skew (“Telban Maritime Storage”)**

- See: Three stories of weathered teak planks gone grey from sea spray, sagging tile roof missing shingles. Faded sign: “Telban Maritime Storage - Est. 839 PD” (Telban = anagram of Caliban). No 6-year-old business would look this decrepit.
- Hear/Smell: Creak of wood, distant gulls, muffled voices from inside. Salt, tar, old fish, and something acrid like ozone.
- Feel: Suspicious, deliberately aged exterior concealing active operations
- Feature: Multiple entry points (front doors with guards, locked side door, rooftop skylight, barred office window). Interior has main storage (60x40ft, high ceiling with rafters for climbing), office with ledgers and strongbox, and hidden back room behind shelf of rope coils containing bottled air elementals, brass brazier with Primordial runes, and ritual components.

**Place of Power in the Sluiceweave (Confluence of Three Waters)**

- See: Circular natural cavern, 40ft diameter with 30ft domed ceiling dripping with mineral formations. Ancient stone pillar (6ft tall, 2ft diameter) on raised platform at center, covered in glowing Primordial script. Four brass censers at cardinal points. Bioluminescent moss (dim green) plus pillar’s blue-white glow plus censer flames (amber).
- Hear/Smell: Rushing water, thrumming vibration, occasional crack of displaced energy. Wet stone, incense (sandalwood and something acrid), ozone.
- Feel: Oppressive, watching, the sense of an ancient thing being wounded. Temperature drops noticeably (10 degrees cooler than surrounding tunnels).
- Feature: Three water channels converge in central whirlpool. Platform 5ft higher than main floor. Water channels 3ft deep, fast-flowing (Athletics DC 12 to cross). Stalactites on ceiling (AC 15, 5 HP, falls deal 2d6 in 5ft radius). Damp stone gives disadvantage on Acrobatics/Athletics. Censers actively draining energy from the ley line anchor.

**The Typhoon’s Rest (Tavern in Restless Wharf)**

- See: Two-story building of salt-stained planks with slate roof and shuttered windows glowing orange. Sign of a ship in a tempest swings on rusted chains. Interior: long oak bar, mix of round and long tables (~35 seating), small stage with halfling bard Pip Silverstring.
- Hear/Smell: Salt air, fried fish, ale, tobacco smoke, wet wood. Raucous laughter, clinking mugs, off-key singing, fiddle playing sailor’s tunes.
- Feel: Rough but not dangerous; sailors unwinding, merchants making deals, locals avoiding questions
- Feature: Back alley with private dock (20ft) for boat deliveries. External stairs to second floor. Room 4 is Caliban’s rental. Cellar has hidden area behind false wine rack (Investigation DC 16) containing smuggled goods, inactive portal circle, and ritual notes in Primordial.

## DM Notes

### Caliban Background

Caliban is a djinn who came to Nicodranas approximately 6 years ago. He’s been secretly building influence through three fronts: smuggling (warehouse), siphoning magical energy (Sluiceweave), and an information network (tavern). His goal is to gather enough power/resources to break free of an ancient binding.

Dazz wants him stopped because Caliban’s meddling with the Sluiceweave weakened the city’s ancient magical defenses, making the tsunami far worse than it should have been. What actually stopped the tsunami was U’kotoa’s intervention. Dazz is trying to maintain balance and prevent further catastrophe, and is also worried about U’kotoa gaining influence.

### Warehouse Details

**Entry Points:**

- Front entrance: Large double doors (12ft high), two guards (Jorik and Mara) playing dice. Challenge anyone: “Got business with Mr. Kallow? He’s expecting you?”
- Side door: Reinforced oak, locked (Thieves’ Tools DC 13), rotted frame (Athletics DC 15 to force). Stealth DC 12 to avoid guard notice.
- Roof access: Climb exterior (Athletics DC 13), skylight (4x4ft, 20ft drop), latch (Thieves’ Tools DC 10). Perception DC 12 to spot Renz in office.
- Office window: 8ft off ground, iron bars (Strength DC 18 to bend), can see Renz working on ledgers.

**Interior:**

- Main storage (60x40ft): High ceiling (25ft), exposed rafters (Athletics DC 12 to climb), dozens of crates. Contraband marked with air elemental symbols on underside (Investigation DC 13 if searching, DC 16 if just passing). Four pillars provide cover. Unstable crates (Dex DC 12 or 1d6 bludgeoning + prone).
- Office (15x20ft): Wooden partition with glass window. Cluttered desk, locked filing cabinet, strongbox under desk (DC 15). Bell on door.
- Secret back room (20x20ft): Hidden door behind rope coils (Investigation DC 15 searching, DC 17 noticing). Opened by pulling specific rope (marked with small knot). Oddly cool and dry (preservation magic). Contains: 3 crates of bottled air elementals, brass brazier with Primordial runes, shipping manifest for “Typhoon’s Rest, midnight,” ritual supplies.

**Warehouse Secrets & Clues:**

- Ledgers (Investigation DC 14): Regular shipments of “wind stones” (bottled air elementals), payment to “C. Stormcaller,” notation about “Sluice channel payment overdue - pressure building.”
- Contraband: 3 crates of bottled air elementals, brass brazier (summoning circle part), manifest showing next delivery tonight at Restless Wharf tavern.
- Strongbox (DC 15 or key from Renz): Map of Nicodranas with three locations marked -- warehouse (X’d out), Sluiceweave (circled multiple times), tavern (star). Primordial notation: “When the channels align, the gate opens.”

### Sluiceweave Details

**Getting There (from Session 5 access point):**

- Iron grate entrance near Open Quay docks (on their previous map). 10ft drop (ladder rungs slick, Dex DC 10). Tunnel 8ft high, 6ft wide, ankle-deep water flowing west. Carved markers every 50ft (History DC 14 for Marquesan numerals).

- Perception DC 12: Strange humming growing louder, resonates in teeth and bones.
- Perception DC 15: Faint blue-white glow ahead, flickering like lightning in clouds.

**The Magical Working:**

- Caliban has been siphoning energy from the ley line nexus. Censers channel power into himself.
- Detect Magic: Pillar = strong abjuration/evocation (ancient city defense). Censers = moderate conjuration (active siphoning). Water = faint transmutation (being altered).
- Arcana DC 15: Recognize ley line confluence; censers draining energy elsewhere.
- Arcana DC 18: City’s magical defenses (including tsunami barrier) draw from this point.

**Sluiceweave Secrets & Clues:**

- Censers: Brass, Primordial script, burning incense mixed with powdered gemstones. Arcana/Religion DC 13: Djinn ritual channeling elemental air energy. Each worth ~100gp.

### The U’kotoa Connection

**What Actually Happened with the Tsunami:**

- Caliban’s 6-year energy drain weakened the city’s defenses (the ley line anchor in the Sluiceweave).
- This made Nicodranas vulnerable to magical assault.
- Someone (possibly U’kotoa cultists?) triggered or amplified a natural tsunami.
- U’kotoa then “saved” the city by creating that offshore barrier.
- This gives U’kotoa credit, worship, and influence.

**Lucien’s Mother -- The Marquess:**

- She is a secret follower/warlock of U’kotoa (possibly Avantika reincarnated?).
- She WANTS U’kotoa to gain power and influence.
- She’s been watching Caliban’s operation through Mara (the guard at warehouse).
- When she learns about the party’s quest: will be suspiciously enthusiastic and supportive, might offer resources/information/aid, could invite them to the Palace, may try to steer them toward confronting Caliban directly (chaos serves her purposes).

**Why the Marquess Supports Stopping Caliban:**

1. Caliban weakened defenses, but the job’s done -- no need for him anymore.
2. His continued presence risks discovery of the larger plot.
3. If party stops Caliban, U’kotoa still gets credit for saving city.
4. Removes a rival power player (djinn vs. leviathan).
5. Party becomes useful tools she can manipulate.

**Red Flags for Players (above the table):**

- Why is a powerful noble so interested in helping adventurers?
- Why does she care about some djinn smuggler?
- Her questions might probe too deeply into their methods/discoveries.
- She might discourage them from investigating the Sluiceweave’s true purpose.
- If Lucien mentions this, she might get very interested in the ley line anchor.

### Roleplaying the Marquess (When She Learns About the Quest)

**When/How She Finds Out:**

**Through Mara (most likely):**

- Mara reports back after warehouse encounter.
- Marquess knows party is pursuing Caliban before they even finish investigating.

**Through Lucien:**

- He might mention it casually at dinner.
- “Oh mother, we’ve been hired to stop some djinn smuggler”
- She has to control her reaction.

**Through Palace Connections:**

- Watch Captain Doria reports adventurers asking questions about warehouses/smuggling.
- Spy network picks up their investigation.

**Her Initial Reaction (in private):**

- Relief: Caliban’s usefulness has ended, now he’s a loose end.
- Calculation: How can I use this?
- Caution: Don’t be too enthusiastic -- that would be suspicious.
- Opportunity: This party could be valuable tools.

**Her Approach to the Party:**

*First Contact:*

- Summon them to Palace “for a private discussion.”
- Formal but warm -- treats them as equals (unusual for her).
- Lucien is present but confused by her interest.
- Offers refreshments, comfortable seating -- wants them relaxed.

*Her Questions (seemingly innocent):*

1. “Who hired you for this task?” (wants to know about Dazz)
2. “What have you discovered so far?” (assessing how much they know)
3. “Did you find anything in the Sluiceweave?” (VERY interested in ley line anchor)
4. “Has this djinn posed a direct threat?” (gauging if they know about tsunami connection)

*Her Offers (TOO generous):*

1. Access to Palace resources (maps, records, “anything you need”)
2. Financial support (200gp “discretionary fund”)
3. Palace guards as backup “if you need muscle”
4. Information from her spy network about Caliban’s movements
5. Legal immunity for any “necessary actions” (blank check for violence)

*Her Suggestions (steering them):*

- “This sounds dangerous -- you should confront him directly, end this quickly”
- “Don’t waste time investigating -- we know he’s guilty”
- “The Sluiceweave sounds unstable -- best to avoid it”
- “I can have my people seal those tunnels after you’re done” (prevent future investigation)

*Red Flags in Her Behavior:*

- Asks about Sluiceweave multiple times
- Discourages thorough investigation
- Pushes for violent confrontation
- Unusually interested in their methods/discoveries
- Wants detailed reports (ostensibly to “help”)
- Avoids questions about city defenses or tsunami
- If they mention “ley line anchor” she goes very still, then changes subject

**Specific Dialogue Examples:**

*Opening:*

> “Thank you for coming. Lucien has told me a little about your… activities. I understand you’re pursuing a dangerous criminal -- this djinn, Caliban?” *leans forward, intense focus* “I want you to know you have the full support of this office.”

*If they’re suspicious:*

> “You seem surprised by my interest. Let me be frank -- Nicodranas nearly drowned a week ago. Any threat to our city’s safety is my direct concern. This Caliban has been operating in MY city, under MY watch. That’s… personal.”

*If they mention the Sluiceweave:*

> *visible tension* “The ancient waterways? Dangerous place. Did you find anything of note down there?” *pause* “Any… unusual structures? Old mechanisms?”

*If they tell her about the ley line anchor:*

> *goes very still* “A ley line anchor. How… fascinating. The ancients did such remarkable work.” *recovers* “Obviously this is critical infrastructure. Perhaps, after Caliban is dealt with, we should discuss securing that location. Can’t have just anyone accessing it.”

*Lucien’s Confusion:*

> Lucien: “Mother, why are you so interested in some smuggler?”
> Marquess: *sharp look* “Because he endangered MY city and MY people. Including you.” *softens* “I won’t have criminals operating with impunity. These heroes are doing the work the guard is too overwhelmed to handle.”

*Her “Generous” Offer:*

> “I’m prepared to offer substantial support. Gold, information, resources. All I ask in return is regular reports and… discretion. Some matters of state are sensitive. I trust you understand.”

**What She DOESN’T Say:**

- Anything about U’kotoa
- That she knew about Caliban before
- That Mara works for her
- Her interest in the weakened defenses
- Connection between tsunami and Sluiceweave
- Her plans for the party after Caliban is gone

**How to Play Suspicion:**

*If Players Push Back:*

- She backs off gracefully.
- “I apologize if I’ve overstepped. I’m simply concerned for the city.”
- Doesn’t rescind offers -- that would be suspicious.
- But becomes more guarded in future interactions.

*If Players Accept Her Help:*

- Provides exactly what she promised (she keeps her word).
- Uses it to track their progress.
- Prepares for next phase of her plans.
- Starts considering them as potential assets vs. potential threats.

*If Lucien Notices:*

- She has private conversation with him later.
- Spins it as “maternal concern for his safety.”
- Might use this to probe what he knows.
- Could create tension between Lucien and party if he sides with her.

**After the Session:**

*If Caliban is Stopped:*

- Publicly praises the party.
- Privately moves to consolidate U’kotoa’s influence.
- Offers them another “job” soon (Session 8 hook).

*If Caliban Escapes:*

- “Disappointed” but understanding.
- Offers continued support for hunting him.
- Secretly doesn’t care -- he’s not her priority anymore.

*Either Way:*

- She’s now actively interested in the party.
- They’re either useful tools or potential problems.
- Sets up complex relationship for future sessions.
